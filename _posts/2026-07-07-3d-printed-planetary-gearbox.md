This project involved designing and building a two stage **16:1 planetary gearbox** for a **NEMA 17 stepper motor**. The goal was to create a reduction gearbox that could be manufactured entirely using 3D printing. I plan on using this later for my stepper motor based robotic arm. 

The gearbox was designed from the ground up. Gear geometry, housing, bearing locations, clearances, and assembly were all developed in Fusion 360 with the intention of producing a transmission that could be assembled directly from printed parts.

During development, multiple design iterations were printed and evaluated to determine appropriate clearances between mating gears. A **0.2 mm backlash** was ultimately selected to provide reliable meshing without binding while still keeping rotational play low.
